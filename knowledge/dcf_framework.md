# DCF Valuation Framework for Growth Stocks

## When to Use DCF
Use DCF for companies with predictable cash flows (SaaS, mature tech). For early-stage hypergrowth companies with negative FCF, use EV/NTM Revenue or EV/NTM Gross Profit multiples instead.

## Key Inputs
- **Revenue growth rate**: Use a 3-stage model (high growth → deceleration → terminal)
- **FCF margin**: Project improvement as the company scales (operating leverage)
- **Discount rate (WACC)**: Typically 10-15% for growth stocks reflecting equity risk premium
- **Terminal growth rate**: 2-4% (GDP-linked) for mature phase

## Rule of Thumb Valuation Shortcuts

### EV/Revenue Multiple Framework
| Revenue Growth | Gross Margin | Fair EV/NTM Rev |
|---------------|--------------|-----------------|
| >50% | >70% | 15-25x |
| 30-50% | >70% | 8-15x |
| 20-30% | >70% | 5-8x |
| 15-20% | >60% | 3-5x |
| <15% | >60% | 1-3x |

### PEG Ratio
- PEG = (P/E) / (EPS growth rate)
- PEG < 1.0: potentially undervalued relative to growth
- PEG 1.0-1.5: fairly valued
- PEG > 2.0: expensive relative to growth
- Most reliable for profitable companies with consistent EPS growth

## Sanity Checks
1. FCF yield > 2% at current price for mature growth stocks
2. EV/GP multiple < 20x for pre-profit companies
3. Cross-check with comparable company multiples
4. Analyst consensus target as independent validation

## Red Flags in Valuation
- Revenue growth decelerating faster than expected
- Gross margin compression (pricing pressure or mix shift)
- Rising sales & marketing as % of revenue (efficiency declining)
- Free cash flow conversion ratio < 70% of net income (earnings quality issue)
