# Stock Analysis Frameworks

## Fundamental Analysis Checklist
When evaluating a stock fundamentally, examine:
1. Revenue growth (YoY and sequential)
2. Gross and net profit margins (expanding or contracting?)
3. Free cash flow generation — more reliable than EPS
4. P/E ratio vs. sector peers and historical average
5. Debt/Equity ratio — flag if D/E > 2 for non-financial companies
6. Return on Equity (ROE) > 15% is generally strong
7. Analyst consensus and price target vs. current price

## Technical Analysis: RSI Interpretation
- RSI > 70: Overbought — potential pullback signal
- RSI < 30: Oversold — potential bounce signal
- RSI 50-70: Bullish momentum
- RSI 30-50: Bearish momentum
- Divergence (price making new highs, RSI not): bearish warning

## MACD Interpretation
- MACD crosses above signal line: bullish signal
- MACD crosses below signal line: bearish signal
- Histogram expanding above zero: strengthening uptrend
- Histogram expanding below zero: strengthening downtrend

## Moving Average Strategy
- Price above SMA 200: Long-term uptrend (bullish bias)
- Price below SMA 200: Long-term downtrend (bearish bias)
- SMA 50 crosses above SMA 200 (Golden Cross): strong bullish signal
- SMA 50 crosses below SMA 200 (Death Cross): strong bearish signal

## Valuation Frameworks

### P/E Ratio Context
- Growth stocks (tech): 25-50 P/E is normal; >50 requires exceptional growth
- Value stocks: P/E < 15 may indicate undervaluation
- Compare to sector average and historical P/E
- Use forward P/E when earnings are recovering

### PEG Ratio (P/E divided by growth rate)
- PEG < 1: potentially undervalued relative to growth
- PEG 1-2: fairly valued
- PEG > 2: potentially overvalued

## Risk Assessment
- Beta > 1.5: high volatility, amplifies market moves
- Beta < 0.8: defensive stock, lower market correlation
- High short interest (>20%): potential short squeeze OR bearish signal
- Insider selling: monitor for unusual patterns

## Sector-Specific Notes

### Technology
- Key metrics: ARR/MRR growth, net revenue retention, CAC/LTV
- Watch for margin expansion as scale increases
- Rate sensitive: high multiple tech stocks decline when rates rise

### Energy
- Oil price correlation is key
- Monitor production costs vs. realized price
- Dividend sustainability: check payout ratio vs. FCF

### Financials
- Net Interest Margin (NIM) expands with rising rates
- Loan loss provisions signal credit quality
- Tier 1 capital ratio for bank safety
