# Tech & SaaS Growth Stock Analysis Framework

## The Rule of 40
Revenue Growth Rate + FCF Margin >= 40% = healthy SaaS business
- Score > 60%: exceptional (NVDA, CRM at peak)
- Score 40-60%: strong
- Score < 40%: underperforming for a SaaS company
- Use NTM (next twelve months) estimates for forward-looking analysis

## Key SaaS Metrics

### Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
- Measures revenue expansion from existing customers
- NRR > 130%: best-in-class (Snowflake, Datadog at peak)
- NRR > 120%: excellent
- NRR > 110%: good
- NRR < 100%: customers are churning or contracting (red flag)

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Payback Period
- < 12 months: excellent efficiency
- 12-24 months: acceptable
- > 24 months: capital inefficient, watch for deterioration

### ARR Growth & Cohort Analysis
- Look for consistent ARR growth with improving cohort retention
- Net new ARR acceleration = positive momentum signal
- Net new ARR deceleration for 2+ quarters = serious concern

## AI Infrastructure Stocks (NVDA, AMD, SMCI, ANET)
Key metrics to track:
- Data center revenue % of total (want >50% for AI pure play)
- Gross margin trend (AI mix should be margin accretive for NVDA)
- Customer concentration risk
- Lead times and backlog as demand indicators
- Hyperscaler capex guidance (MSFT, GOOG, META, AMZN) as proxy for demand

## Semiconductor Cycle Analysis
- Semi stocks are highly cyclical — always identify where we are in the cycle
- Inventory correction phase: revenue declining, margins compressed
- Early recovery: book-to-bill ratio > 1.0, lead times extending
- Upcycle: pricing power, margin expansion, beat-and-raise quarters
- Peak cycle risk: inventory buildup at customers, guidance cuts incoming

## SaaS Valuation Framework
- Pre-profit: EV/NTM Revenue is primary metric
- Approaching profitability: EV/NTM Gross Profit
- Profitable: EV/FCF or P/FCF
- Best-in-class (NRR>120%, Rule of 40 >60%): deserves 15-25x NTM revenue
- Average (NRR 110-120%, R40 40-60%): deserves 8-15x NTM revenue

## Warning Signs in Tech
- Sales cycle elongation (mentioned in earnings call)
- Seat-based license contraction
- Competitive displacement by AI-native alternatives
- Gross margin below 60% for software company
- S&M as % of revenue increasing without corresponding growth acceleration
