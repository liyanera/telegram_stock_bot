# Macro Environment Playbook for Growth Stocks

## Interest Rate Impact on Growth Stocks
Growth stocks are long-duration assets — their value is in future cash flows, making them highly rate-sensitive.

### Rising Rate Environment
- High-multiple growth stocks de-rate (P/E compression)
- Profitable growth stocks outperform unprofitable ones
- Value and dividend stocks relatively outperform
- Defensive playbook: focus on Rule of 40 > 50, positive FCF, low leverage

### Falling Rate Environment
- Growth stocks re-rate (multiple expansion)
- Speculative/unprofitable tech outperforms in early rate-cut cycle
- Best time to accumulate high-growth, high-multiple names
- Aggressive playbook: early-stage SaaS, AI infrastructure, biotech

### Neutral/Plateau Environment (current 2026 context)
- Stock-picking matters more than macro tailwinds
- Focus on earnings quality and execution
- Sector rotation based on relative growth and valuation

## Sector Rotation Framework

### Risk-On Rotation (Fed cutting, VIX falling)
Money flows: Cash → Bonds → Large cap growth → Small cap → Speculative tech

### Risk-Off Rotation (Fed hiking, VIX rising)
Money flows: Speculative → Profitable growth → Large cap defensive → Cash

### AI Supercycle Context (2024-2026)
- AI infrastructure buildout is multi-year, rate-independent capex commitment
- Beneficiaries: NVDA, ANET, SMCI, power/cooling stocks, hyperscalers
- AI software layer monetization is the next wave (2025-2027)
- Key risk: AI capex ROI scrutiny if productivity gains disappoint

## Macro Indicators to Watch

| Indicator | Bullish for Growth | Bearish for Growth |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 10Y Treasury Yield | Falling or stable <4.5% | Rising >5% |
| Fed Funds Rate | Cutting cycle | Hiking cycle |
| Dollar (DXY) | Weakening | Strengthening (hurts multinational EPS) |
| VIX | Below 20 | Above 25 |
| Credit spreads | Tightening | Widening |
| PMI | >50 and rising | <50 or falling |

## Positioning by Environment

### Soft Landing (base case 2026)
- Overweight: AI infrastructure, profitable SaaS, financials
- Underweight: Rate-sensitive unprofitable growth, real estate

### Hard Landing / Recession
- Overweight: Cash, Treasuries, defensive tech (MSFT, GOOG)
- Underweight: High-multiple growth, cyclical semis, consumer discretionary

### Stagflation (worst case)
- Overweight: Energy, commodities, pricing-power businesses
- Underweight: Long-duration growth assets across the board

## Earnings Season Macro Calendar
- Jan/Feb: Q4 earnings — sets tone for the year
- Apr/May: Q1 earnings — first read on economic momentum
- Jul/Aug: Q2 earnings — midyear check, often volatile
- Oct/Nov: Q3 earnings — sets up year-end positioning
