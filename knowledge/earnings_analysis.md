# Earnings Analysis Playbook

## Pre-Earnings Checklist
1. What are consensus revenue and EPS estimates?
2. What guidance did management give last quarter?
3. What is the options market implying (implied move)?
4. What has the stock done YTD vs sector? (stretched = higher bar)
5. Any recent analyst estimate revisions? (positive revisions = tailwind)

## Reading an Earnings Report: What Actually Matters

### Tier 1 (Most Important)
- **Revenue vs consensus**: Beat/miss AND the magnitude
- **Forward guidance**: Raised, maintained, or lowered? This drives the stock
- **Gross margin**: Expanding = pricing power + scale. Contracting = red flag
- **Operating leverage**: Revenue growing faster than expenses = positive

### Tier 2 (Important Context)
- **EPS**: Often distorted by buybacks or one-time items; less reliable than revenue
- **Free cash flow**: Most honest measure of business health
- **Key operating metric**: MAU, ARR, GMV, NRR — depends on business model

### Tier 3 (Color/Context)
- Management commentary on demand environment
- Geographic mix (US vs international)
- Customer count and segment breakdown

## Interpreting Guidance
- **Beat revenue + raise guidance**: Strong buy signal — this is the "beat and raise" pattern
- **Beat revenue + maintain guidance**: Neutral to slightly positive
- **Beat revenue + lower guidance**: Dangerous — "kitchen sink" or demand deterioration
- **Miss revenue + lower guidance**: Avoid, likely more downside ahead

## Post-Earnings Price Action Patterns
- Gap up on beat → consolidates above gap = institutional accumulation, hold
- Gap up on beat → fills the gap within a week = weak hands, caution
- Gap down on miss → recovers same day = selling exhausted, potential buy
- Gap down on miss → continues lower = follow-through selling, avoid

## Earnings Call Red Flags
- Management avoids giving specific guidance ("too much uncertainty")
- CFO mentions "elongated sales cycles"
- Sudden departure of CEO/CFO announced alongside earnings
- Significant increase in days sales outstanding (DSO)
- Shift from subscription to usage-based language (often signals deceleration)

## Sector-Specific Earnings Focus

### Cloud/SaaS
Primary: NRR, ARR growth, RPO (remaining performance obligations)

### Semiconductors
Primary: Data center revenue, gross margin, inventory levels, book-to-bill

### E-commerce/Marketplace
Primary: GMV growth, take rate, EBITDA margin

### Fintech
Primary: Revenue per user, transaction volume, credit quality (charge-off rates)
